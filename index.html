Have your name inside the temple

Ever since ancient times, in both East and West, a tradition has been followed of placing the names of benefactors inside temples and sacred shrines.

The banks of the Yamuna River in Vrindavan are covered with dozens of names written on stones. These are all the names of the Vaishnava benefactors who took care of this pilgrimage place with their financial support.

Even in some churches you can see memorial desks or even little shrines devoted to people who supported the building or services there, and the same tradition can be seen in other ancient religions, too.

The aim of it is very simple and very deep in its meaning. The name represents the inner or energetic connection that its owner has built into the temple by supporting it with material donations. Whenever we give money or some other material offerings, it represents a very dense energy. It connects us with that person or that place on a very basic, fundamental level. This person becomes part of it and automatically participates in every prayer in the temple, thus sharing the spiritual fruits of each temple activity.

In India, Swami told the group there with him: “You all have come here now, because, for sure, in one life you all were here, participating in building this temple.”

Divine Mother is there for all who participate, no matter how much they have donated. The Hindu tradition says that whenever you give to Supreme Mother, She will multiply what you have given and return it back to you. And She will always take care and call back all Her children. Sri Maha Lakshmi and Sri Sri Radha – the purest, sweetest and highest aspects of Her – will take Their abode in the new temple of Shree Peetha Nilaya and give Their eternal blessings to all who help Their home to be built. Even if you are not physically there, your energy will be present in the temple, through your name, for all time.

Three Stages of Construction

Construction of the Sri Kripeshwarnath Temple will happen in three stages. The three stages combined are expected to require funding of up to 2.5m €.

First, the new temple will be built, on the ground floor level.

This is the 'main' stage of construction, where a temple large enough for many guests will be built. Deities will be installed on the altar in this room, and the names of the people who have donated will be installed on this altar. The total amount for this stage of construction will be 800.000€, and construction is expected to be completed by Summer 2017.

Second, the darshan hall upstairs will be renovated and expanded.

The upstairs hall that we have experienced in wonderful events such as the Shreemad Bhagavad Gita course, and countless darshans and satsangs with Guruji, will be expanded: the front entrance room will be joined with the darshan hall itself, creating a larger space for events. This room will continue to host darshans, and also will allow for an increased amount of guests to be with Guruji in person during events.

Third, the access and beautifications of the exterior of the building will be improved.

This stage of construction will include stairways, walkways, and ramps to the new temple and darshan hall, and the development of gardens.
